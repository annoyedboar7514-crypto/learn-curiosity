LEARN CURIOSITY — "CROSSROADS" STORY MODE  FINAL LOCKED SPEC v1.1 + Build Prompt  July 2026 | Supersedes v1.0. Decisions locked: in-app only (behind login), landmark unlock with permanent replayability.  1. THE MODE IN ONE PARAGRAPH  Crossroads is a voice-first branching story mode built on real moments from history, living inside the app behind login. The child's mentor narrates a historically grounded story that stops at genuine decision points — would-you-rather choices with real stakes. The child chooses and must say WHY before the story continues; the story branches; consequences play out with realistic, sometimes delayed cause and effect. One path through every story is what actually happened in history, revealed only at the end. Rewinding and replaying is encouraged and framed as a superpower — counterfactual thinking is the learning mechanism. Each story teaches one named thinking concept (risk, reward, cause-and-effect, tradeoff, sunk cost, irreversibility). Some stories have no winning path — only different losses, handled differently — because that is what some of life is.  2. ACCESS MODEL (LOCKED)  -   Landmark unlock:   Crossroads stories appear as distinct landmark cards along the level path — roughly one per 4-5 regular levels. A child reaches the landmark by completing the levels before it. The landmark is visually special ("A crossroads in history is ahead") and era-matched.  -   Permanently replayable after unlock:   once a child has reached a crossroads, it stays open forever. Unlocked crossroads render as glowing waypoints on the Story Map the child can return to any time. This preserves pacing (kids can't binge ahead) while maximizing the rewind behavior the mode is built on.  -   Replay ≠ the daily lesson.   A first playthrough of a newly reached crossroads counts as that day's session. Replays are extra — available after the day's regular lesson, or freely on weekends (parent-configurable later; default: replays always available). Replays never block or substitute the level sequence beyond the first playthrough.

3. STORY STRUCTURE — THE DIAMOND (locked)  -   3-4 decision points per story; binary would-you-rather for K-2, binary-plus-wildcard-third for 5-6  -   Diverge-then-reconverge: 10-14 authored scene nodes, 3-4 distinct endings — never an exponential tree  -   Choices are dilemmas, not puzzles: both options genuinely defensible (test: would two smart adults disagree?)  -   Stakes stated aloud before every choice — risk/reward vocabulary modeled at every node  -   Consequences delayed and realistic; a choice at decision 1 can echo at decision 3  -   The True Path:   exactly one route is what history records, hidden until first completion, then revealed on the path map with a short "what really happened" telling. Where historians disagree, the mentor says so plainly.  4. THE WHY-GATE (locked — this is the pedagogy)  After every choice, the mentor asks "why?" and waits for a spoken (or typed-fallback) answer before the story advances. No reasonless clicking through branches. Why-quotes are stored per run, referenced in consequence callbacks ("You said speed mattered most. It just cost you the water barrels."), quoted in the debrief, and surfaced in the parent summary.  5. THE CONCEPT LAYER  One primary concept tag per story, named in plain language by the mentor exactly once, in the debrief, after the child has lived it:  Concept   Child-facing framing  Risk vs. reward   "What you might lose vs. what you might gain  — and you never know for sure"  Cause and effect   "Choices are dominoes — the fall starts  quietly"  Tradeoff   "Sometimes choosing one good thing means  losing another good thing"  Sunk cost   "'We've come too far to stop' — is that a  reason, or a trap?"  Irreversibility   "Some doors lock behind you"

Concept   Child-facing framing  Win-loss vs. loss-loss   "Some crossroads have no winning path —  only choosing what you can live with losing"  6. NO-WIN STORIES (locked rules)  -   Marked   no_win: true   in content; never announced to the child — discovering it is the lesson  -   Age-gated: K-2 always has a warm-if-costly path; 3-4 gets recoverable-loss dilemmas; 5-6 gets the full weight (Apollo 13, Shackleton, Socrates)  -   Debrief opener after a no-win ending: "You couldn't win that one. Nobody could. So what were you actually choosing between?"  -   Rewinding after a no-win ending is explicitly encouraged — finding that the other path ALSO loses (differently) is when the concept locks in  -   Tone register locked:   loss, met well.   Never gratuitous, never graphic, never hopeless. Apollo 13 — a triumph inside a failure — is the emotional north star.  7. REWIND & REPLAY BEHAVIOR (updated for permanent replayability)  -   Path Map after every ending:   child's route lit gold, unexplored branches grey, prior-run routes footprint-marked, True Path as a teal thread after first completion. No-win maps show whatWasLost on each reached ending.  -   Mid-story rewind:   jump to any previously visited decision node; state forks into a new run; unlimited.  -   Cross-run memory:   the mentor remembers prior runs in structured form (choices + why-quotes only, consistent with the memory policy in Master Blueprint §5.2). Returning to a story weeks later: "Last time you turned back at the strait. Still feel that way?"  -   Comparative debriefs on replays:   replays skip the full first-run debrief and run a shorter comparative one instead — 2-3 questions contrasting this run against the last ("You chose differently this time. What changed your mind?" "Which ending was better — and better for WHOM?"). Comparing your own past reasoning to your present reasoning is Pillar 5 in its purest form, and it keeps replays snappy (10-12 min vs. 20).  -   Full-map completion:   no badge, no confetti. The fully lit map itself is the artifact; it's saved to the child's Curiosity Journal and noted once, warmly, by the mentor.  -   Framing lines (system prompt canon): "Here's the thing about history — [name] never got to go back. You do. That's your superpower in here." / "Repicking isn't cheating. It's what thinking looks like."

8. COST MODEL (new — replayability makes this matter)  Narration scene nodes are static per age band →   generate TTS audio once per node per age band per mentor voice and cache it as audio files.   Replays then cost near-zero for narration; only the why-exchanges and the debrief hit live STT/LLM/TTS. Estimated: first playthrough ≈ a normal session ($0.20-0.40); replays ≈ $0.05-0.10. Cache at build/publish time, not on first play, so first-play latency is also better than regular lessons. This is the engineering decision that makes "replayable forever" financially safe.  9. CLAUDE CODE PROMPT — CROSSROADS ENGINE (v1.1, replaces v1.0 prompt)  Read /docs first — design system, voice pivot plan, and Crossroads FINAL SPEC v1.1 are canonical. Build the Crossroads branching story engine inside the existing logged-in session experience. This is in-app only; nothing renders on public routes.  1. Content schema.   TypeScript schema:   CrossroadsStory { id, era, title, pillarTag, conceptTag, conceptFraming, noWin, ageBands, landmarkAfterLevel, truePathNodeIds, nodes[] } ;   StoryNode { id, type: 'scene'|'decision'|'ending', narrationByBand, panelImageRef, cachedAudioRefs, choices?: [{ id, label, stakesLine, nextNodeId, isTruePath }], consequenceEchoes?: [{ triggeredByChoiceId, narrationInsert }], endingSummary?: { whatWasLost, whatWasGained } } . Build-time validation: diamond shape (3-4 decisions, 10-14 nodes, 3-4 endings, all paths terminate, exactly one complete true path), every decision has defensible stakes lines, noWin stories have no ending where whatWasLost is empty.

2. Audio caching pipeline.   A publish script that pre-generates TTS audio for every scene/ending narration per age band per mentor voice and stores the files (Vercel blob or /public/audio); the player streams cached audio for narration and only uses live TTS for dynamic mentor speech (why-gate reactions, callbacks, debrief). This is required, not optional — it's what makes unlimited replays affordable.  3. Story player.   Reuse existing session components (transcript, panels, push-to-talk). Node flow per spec §9. Hard requirement: the why-gate — after every choice the mentor asks why and blocks story advance until a spoken or typed reason is captured and logged. Consequence-echo inserts fire when the triggering choice is on the current run's path.  4. Run state, rewind, cross-run memory.   Persist per child per story: runs[] with path, choice+why pairs, ending, rewind count, timestamps. Mid-story rewind to any visited decision forks a new run. On story open, inject structured prior-run summary (choices + whys only, no freeform) into the mentor context for "last time you..." callbacks.  5. Path Map.   SVG diamond in brand tokens (teal #1B6E6B, gold #E8A33D, cream #FBF6EC, charcoal #233137, Fraunces): current route gold with draw-in animation, unexplored grey, prior-run routes footprint-marked, True Path teal thread after first completion + "what really happened" panel, per-ending whatWasLost lines on noWin stories. No scores, badges, or percentages; a fully explored map glows and writes a journal record.  6. Debriefs.   First completion: full debrief on the standard conversation engine — pillar question bank + conceptFraming (named exactly once) + this run's why-quotes + noWin opener rule from spec §6. Replays: comparative debrief, 2-3 questions contrasting current vs. previous run using both runs' why-quotes, target 10-12 minute total replay session.  7. Landmarks + replayability.   Crossroads landmark cards render in the level sequence at landmarkAfterLevel, styled as special events; first completion advances progression like a level. After unlock, the story remains permanently accessible from the Story Map as a glowing waypoint. Enforce ageBands gating — a child outside the band never sees the landmark. First-play-today vs. replay logic per spec §2.  8. Logging + parent summary.   Extend session logs with the crossroads_run object; extend the parent summary template per spec §11 including the replay variant.
